Summary

Boston real-estate in 2000-2022 can be characterized by consistent increase in the price of property. The most pervasive transaction type was the condominium, followed by 1-family residents and apartments. The popularity of condominium has stayed consistent since 2000. The popularity of of the rest of the residential property types have similarly stayed consistent.

The Homes for Profit article spotlighted the concern of speculative investments in Boston, specifically property flipping: defined as the act of selling a property less than two years of having bought it with the purpose of profit. Flipped properties make up only 3.75% of the 49,301 transactions recorded between 2000-2023. The flipped properties are mainly located in downtown Boston. The database aggregates the flipped properties by the time difference between the purchase and sell date: short (less than 6 months), medium (6 - 12 months), and long (12-24 months). The most frequent flip term was long-term, which seems to have risen to popularity starting 2010. The long-term flips also contained the more profittable residential flips (see Fig. 8), which were often located in Back Bay and South End.

Overall, the data shows that the price of homes and profit per transaction projected to continue increasing. Without regulation, there likely will be no sudden decrease in prevalence of flipping.
